II. ELIGIBILITY FOR ADMISSION
9. (a) A person possessing the Degree of Homoeopathy (Bachelor of Homoeopathy Medicine
and Surgery) from the Tamil Nadu Dr. MGR Medical University or from any other
recognized University or Board or Medical Institution specified in erstwhile Indian
Medicine Central Council Act 1970 or National Commission for Indian System of
Medicine Act, 2020, and enrolled in Central Counsil of Indian System of Medicine or
Tamil Nadu Homoeopathy Medical Council, with successful completion of CRRI before
31.08.2025 by the NCH / AIAPGET-2025.
(b) The candidates should fulfil the eligibility criteria for applying AIAPGET 2025; should
have qualified in AIAPGET 2025 and should have obtained minimum of marks at 50th
percentile. Provided that in respect of candidates belonging to the Scheduled Castes,
Scheduled Tribes, Most Backward Classes and Denotified Community, Backward Classes
(Muslim) and Backward Classes; the minimum marks shall be at 40th percentile; for
candidates with benchmark disabilities specified under the Rights of persons with
disabilities Act, 2016, the minimum marks shall be at 45th percentile for the General
Category and 40th percentile for the Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Most Backward
Classes and Denotified Community, Backward Classes (Muslim) and Backward classes.
Note: Provided further that when sufficient number of candidates in the respective
categories fail to secure minimum marks in the All India AYUSH Post graduate Entrance
Test (AIAPGET), as specified above, held for any academic year for admission to Post –
Graduate courses, the Central Government in consultation with the Council may at its
discretion lower the minimum marks required for admission to Post – Graduate course for
candidates belonging to respective categories and marks so lowered by the Central
Government shall be applicable for that academic year only.
10. The Candidate should be a Citizen of India and should have undergone B.H.M.S. Course.
Candidates who have passed B.H.M.S from other University should upload an Eligibility
Certificate received from the Tamil Nadu Dr. M.G.R. Medical University and submit the
original at the time of joining in the College.

33. BOND AND DISCONTINUATION RULES FOR M.D. (HOMOEOPATHY) COURSE
a. All Candidates undergoing the M.D. (Homoeopathy) Course should execute a Bond
to the effect that in the event of discontinuing their studies before completion of the
course, along with two sureties in permanent Government Service of equal rank or
higher rank at the time of admission to the course.
b. Further, the Candidates who discontinue the course on or after the last date of
allotment of seats pertaining to the academic session 2025-2026 as fixed by Ministry
of AYUSH / Affiliating University, shall pay the Discontinuation Fees besides
forgoing Tuition Fees to the Homoeopathy Medical Colleges concerned a sum of
Rs.10,00,000/- (Rupees Ten Lakhs only) by means of Demand Draft drawn in favour
of “The Director of Indian Medicine and Homoeopathy, Chennai -106” payable at
Chennai. Unless the penalty amount is paid in total, the Candidates will not be
relieved and original certificates / documents will be retained by the college
authorities. All candidates shall execute an Agreement Bond as per Annexure-III, to
this effect at the time of admission to the College.
c. Forfeiture of Tuition and Other Fees:
Candidates pursuing M.D.(Homoeopathy) course in the event of discontinuing their
studies on or after the cut-off date for admission processes relevant to the particular
admission session may also have to forego the Tuition fees and other kinds of fees
remitted to the college authority during the period of study. This applies even if the
discontinuation is due to personal, medical, or professional reasons. The candidates
who discontinue their study before the completion of M.D.(Homoeopathy) course are
liable to fulfil the above conditions. Hence, Candidates are advised to thoroughly
understand the bond conditions and evaluate their commitment before accepting
admission, as the above rules will be strictly enforced.

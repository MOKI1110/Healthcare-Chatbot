II. ELIGIBILITY FOR ADMISSION
9. a) Candidate who has passed in BNYS Regular Degree Course conducted by the
Tamil Nadu Dr. M.G.R. Medical University / any other University recognized
by UGC under regular stream or a pass in N.D.(Osm) under regular stream.
b) Candidate should have successfully completed the Compulsory Rotatory
Residential Internship (CRRI) and should have registered their names with the
Tamil Nadu Board of Indian Medicine, Chennai-106 or its equivalent State
registration board on or before 31.08.2025.
c) Candidate should be a Citizen of India and should have undergone B.N.Y.S
Degree Course.
d) Candidates who have passed B.N.Y.S / N. D (Osm) in other States or
University should upload an Eligibility Certificate from The Tamil Nadu Dr.
M.G.R. Medical University and submit the original at the time of joining in the
College.
e) Candidates who are native of other State, but studied the BNYS Course in
6
Tamil Nadu will be considered under the Open Category only.
f) Community Certificates issued by other States will not be considered and such
Candidates will be treated under the Open Category only.
g) Tamil Nadu Candidates who are claiming the eligibility based on their
community, should upload the following two certificates,
i. Community Certificate issued by competent authority from Revenue
Department of Tamil Nadu Government with official seal.
ii. Community Certificate of either of their Parents issued by competent
authority from Revenue Department of Tamil Nadu Government with
official seal.

IV.MODE OF SELECTION AND ADMISSION
14. ENTRANCE EXAMINATION
i. The Entrance Examination for admission to the Post Graduate M.D. (Yoga &
Naturopathy) Course will be conducted as per existing Curriculum / Syllabus
of the Tamil Nadu Dr. M. G. R. Medical University for BNYS Medical
Degree Course.
ii. The Entrance Examination will be covering all subjects and will contain
200 Objective type questions each with four responses (A, B, C, D) in
English.
iii. Each question will carry 0.5 (half) mark. There will be NO NEGATIVE
MARK for incorrect responses. No mark will be given for questions not
answered. When more than one answer is indicated or when it is illegible, no
mark will be awarded for that answer.
15. Admission to the Course will be made purely on merit, based on the marks
obtained in the entrance Examination. Maximum marks for written Entrance
examination will be computed for 100 marks.
16. i. Candidate should bring two copies of downloaded Hall Ticket for the
entrance exam. One copy of Hall ticket should be submitted to the Hall
Superintendent at the time of examination.
ii. An eligible Candidate can get the Application Register Number i.e., A.R. No
from the eligible list that will be uploaded in the official website well before
the Entrance Examination. Admission to the entrance examination will not
confer any rights to the Candidate for admission to the Course if found
ineligible later. Candidates will not be allowed to appear for the Entrance
Examination unless he / she holds the Hall Ticket. The name, address (in

process. Failure to follow instructions will result in expulsion of the
Candidate.

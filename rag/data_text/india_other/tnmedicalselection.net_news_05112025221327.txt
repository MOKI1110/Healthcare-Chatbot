II. ELIGIBILITY FOR ADMISSION
10. a. A person possessing the Degree of Homoeopathy (Bachelor of Homoeopathy Medicine
and Surgery) from the Tamil Nadu Dr. MGR Medical University or from any other
recognized University or Board or Medical Institution specified in erstwhile Indian
Medicine Central Council Act 1970 or National Commission for Indian System of
Medicine Act, 2020, and enrolled in Central Council of Homoeopathy / NCH or Tamil
Nadu Homoeopathy Medical Council, with successful completion of CRRI before
31.08.2025 fixed by the NCH / AIAPGET-2025.
b. The candidates should fulfill the eligibility criteria for applying AIAPGET 2025; should
have qualified in AIAPGET 2025 and should have obtained minimum of marks at 50th
percentile. Provided that in respect of candidates belonging to the Scheduled Castes,
Scheduled Tribes, Most Backward Classes and Denotified Community, Backward Classes
(Muslim) and Backward Classes; the minimum marks shall be at 40th percentile; for
candidates with benchmark disabilities specified under the Rights of persons with
disabilities Act, 2016, the minimum marks shall be at 45th percentile for the General
Category and 40th percentile for the Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Most Backward
Classes and Denotified Community, Backward Classes (Muslim) and Backward classes.
Note: Provided further that when sufficient number of candidates in the respective categories
fail to secure minimum marks in the All India AYUSH Post graduate Entrance Test

applicable for that academic year only.

III. DEFINITION OF SERVICE CAND IDATES & NON-SERVICE CANDIDATES
15. SERVICE CANDIDATES
The following categories of Homoeopathy Medical Graduates only will be treated as
Service candidates and considered for selection against 50% of seats reserved
exclusively for Service Candidates.
(i) Homoeopathy Medical Graduates who were selected by the Tamil Nadu Public
Service Commission / Medical Services Recruitment Board and other regularized service
candidates and appointed in the Tamil Nadu Medical Service on regular basis, who have put
in minimum of TWO years satisfactory and continuous service excluding leave period such
as Earned Leave, Unearned Leave on Medical Certificate, Unearned Leave on Private
Affairs, Maternity Leave and any other unauthorized absence, as on 31.08.2025 either in
Tamil Nadu Medical Service or local bodies and State Organizations.
(ii) Candidates above 50 years as on 31.08.2025 are not eligible to apply.
(iii) Homoeopathy Medical Graduates who are approved Probationers and who are
working
a. As Medical Officers working (continuously for two years) in Local Bodies in Tamil
Nadu. A Doctor absorbed from the Panchayat Union into Government Service as per
G.O.Ms. No 435 / H&FW / IMI-2 Dept. dated: 23-11-2000 will be considered as
Service Candidates provided that they should have completed 2 years of service.
8
b. In Government of India Institutions in the State of Tamil Nādu.
c. In Public Sector Undertaking and Organizations under the Control of Government of
India in Tamil Nadu.
d. In undertaking or organization of Government of Tamil Nadu.
Note: The candidates under Clause 15, sub clause (ii) item (a) to (d) above should give an
undertaking to the concerned authorities that they will serve for 5 years in their respective
institutions after the completion of course. They should produce a certificate from the
concerned authority to show that they have given such an undertaking as per Tamil Nadu
Government rules.
16. NON-SERVICECANDIDATES:
The following category of Homoeopathy Medical Graduates will be treated as Non-Service
Candidates
a. Private Homoeopathy Practitioners Registered under Tamil Nadu Board of Indian
Medicine.
b. Those service candidates selected by TNPSC / MSRB who have put in less than two
years of continuous regular service as on 31.08.2025 will be treated as non-Service
candidates.
c. All others who have put in less than two years of State Government Service or who
are working in Central Government, Public Sector Undertaking, Central
Government Health Scheme (CGHS), Local Bodies, National Rural Health Mission
and Municipalities in Tamil Nadu and Tamil Nadu Government Undertakings and
Organizations will be treated as non-Service candidates.

A Provisional Rank List of eligible candidates will be prepared based on the marks and rank

Tie-Breaking Criteria (Inter-se Merit):
If two or more candidates have secured the same number of marks, the tie shall be resolved and
inter-se merit shall be determined based on the higher rank in AIAPGET 2025 (Homoeopathy).
21. i) Non-Service candidates:
Admission to the course will be made purely on merit, based on the marks obtained in the
AIAPGET 2025 (Homoeopathy).
ii) Service Candidates:
Admission to the course will be made based on marks obtained in AIAPGET 2025
(Homoeopathy) with additional weightage marks awarded to eligible in-service Candidates.
One mark will be awarded for each completed year of Government Service. Two marks will
be awarded for each completed year of Government service in Hill areas and it will be limited
to maximum of 10 marks, as per G.O.(Ms)No.320 Health and Family Welfare [IM 1-2]
Department, dated: 26.09.2023.

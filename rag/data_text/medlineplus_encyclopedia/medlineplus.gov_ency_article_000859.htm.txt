Gumma
To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript.
A gumma is a soft, tumor-like growth of the tissues (granuloma) that occurs in people with syphilis.
Causes
A gumma is caused by the bacteria that cause
syphilis
. It appears during late-stage tertiary syphilis. It most often contains a mass of dead and swollen fiber-like tissue. It is most often seen in the liver. It also can occur in the:
Bone
Brain
Heart
Skin
Testis
Eyes
Similar-looking sores sometimes occur with
tuberculosis
.

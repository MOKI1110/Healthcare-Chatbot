Scurvy
To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript.
Scurvy is a disease that occurs when you have a severe lack of vitamin C (ascorbic acid) in your diet. Scurvy causes general weakness,
anemia
, gum disease, poor wound healing, and
skin hemorrhages
.
Scurvy is rare in the United States. Older adults who are not getting proper nutrition are most affected by scurvy.
Alternative Names
Vitamin C deficiency; Deficiency - vitamin C; Scorbutus

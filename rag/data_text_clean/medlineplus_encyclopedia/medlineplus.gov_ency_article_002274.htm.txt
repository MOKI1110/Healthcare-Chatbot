Physical examination
During a physical examination, a health care provider checks your body to determine if you do or do not have a physical problem.
A physical examination usually includes:
Inspection
(looking at the body)
Palpation
(feeling the body with fingers or hands)
Auscultation
(listening to sounds, usually with a stethoscope)
Percussion
(producing sounds, usually by tapping on specific areas of the body)
